# Star Lanes is Back — In Memory of Uncle Effie Haddad

Dear Friends & Family,

I'm thrilled to share something that's been a labor of love — **The Star Lanes Game** is back, reimagined for the modern web.

Many of you will remember Uncle Effie Haddad, who in 1985 sat down at his computer and created an extraordinary game in GW-BASIC called *Star Lanes — An Interstellar Commerce Game*. It was a game of strategy, luck, and friendly rivalry. We'd huddle around that old PC, watching the screen fill with stars and shipping companies, arguing over stock trades, and celebrating when a well-timed merger made us rich. Those were some of the best moments we shared together.

Uncle Effie may no longer be with us, but his creation lives on.

**Star Lanes has been rebuilt from the ground up** — faithful to the original, but now playable in any web browser, from anywhere in the world:

- **Play Online** — Create a game room and invite friends. Each player takes turns from their own browser, no matter where they are.
- **Real-time Multiplayer** — Watch the board update live as others play. Join as a spectator to cheer from the sidelines.
- **The Sounds You Remember** — The siren, the bell, the short alarm — all recreated from the original BASIC code, note for note.
- **Global Leaderboard** — Compete for bragging rights. Every online win is recorded for posterity.
- **Demo Mode** — Watch the AI play a full game to learn the ropes (or just enjoy the show).

Every detail matters. The 14x28 star map, the 26 shipping companies from Altair Starways to Zapping Megic Co., the mergers, the stock splits, the traps that only activate when Company Z is formed — it's all there, exactly as Uncle Effie designed it.

**How to play:**

Visit the game at: **http://lanes.live**

Sign in with Google, create a room, and share the 4-letter code with your opponents. That's it.

For those of you who remember the original, I hope this brings back warm memories. For those discovering it for the first time, I hope you'll understand why we loved it so much.

This one's for you, Uncle Effie. Thank you for the games, the laughter, and the inspiration.

With love,
Yoseph
